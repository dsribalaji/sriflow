# Interview Script — Managers (S2, S3)

## Project: Personal Finance Tracker Web App
## Date: 2026-06-28
## Duration: 45 minutes
## Goal: Design approval workflow

---

## Opening (5 min)

"Thanks for meeting. I need to understand how you approve budgets and what information you need to make decisions. No wrong answers — I need the reality."

---

## Layer 1 — EXPLORE (15 min)

### Q1: Walk me through your current budget approval process.
**Probe:** How do team members request budget? How do you review? What's the turnaround time?

**Expected Answer:** Probably informal — email or chat messages.

### Q2: What information do you need to approve a budget?
**Probe:** What context helps you decide? What's the minimum info required?

**Expected Answer:** Amount, category, justification, history.

### Q3: What hurts about the current process?
**Probe:** What's the most frustrating part? What takes too long?

**Expected Answer:** Lack of visibility, manual tracking, no audit trail.

---

## Layer 2 — CONTEXT (10 min)

### Q4: Why does budget approval matter?
**Probe:** Is this about cost control? Compliance? Team management?

**Expected Answer:** Cost control + team visibility.

### Q5: Who else is involved in budget decisions?
**Probe:** Do you consult anyone? Do you report to anyone?

**Expected Answer:** Self-contained — managers have authority.

---

## Layer 3 — PROCESS (15 min)

### Q6: What's your ideal approval workflow?
**Probe:** How should a request come to you? What's the ideal turnaround time?

**Expected Answer:** Notification → review → approve/reject → feedback.

### Q7: What happens if you're unavailable?
**Probe:** Who approves in your absence? What's the escalation path?

**Expected Answer:** Auto-approve after X days, or delegate to other manager.

### Q8: What information should approval history contain?
**Probe:** What audit trail do you need? Who can see approval history?

**Expected Answer:** Requester, amount, category, approval date, approver, notes.

---

## Layer 4 — IDEA/FUTURE (10 min)

### Q9: What would "better" look like?
**Probe:** If this app worked perfectly, what would your approval routine be?

**Expected Answer:** One-click approval, automatic notifications, clear history.

### Q10: What's missing from your current process?
**Probe:** What feature would make this a must-have for you?

**Expected Answer:** Mobile approval, batch approval, budget dashboards.

---

## Closing (5 min)

"What did I miss? What question should I have asked that I didn't?"

---

## Simulated Answers (Based on Project Context)

| Question | Simulated Answer |
|----------|------------------|
| Q1 | "People message me. I check the spreadsheet. It takes a day or two." |
| Q2 | "Amount, category, why they need it. History of their spending." |
| Q3 | "I forget to check messages. No visibility into team spending." |
| Q4 | "I need to control costs. Make sure we stay on budget." |
| Q5 | "Just me and the other manager. We split approval by category." |
| Q6 | "Push notification → review → one-click approve → done." |
| Q7 | "Auto-approve after 48 hours. Or the other manager covers." |
| Q8 | "Who requested, amount, category, when approved, who approved, notes." |
| Q9 | "Mobile approval. Batch approve. See team spending at a glance." |
| Q10 | "Mobile notifications. Budget dashboards. Quick approve." |

---

## Key Findings

1. **Current State:** Informal message-based approval, no visibility, manual tracking
2. **Pain Points:** Forgetting to check, no audit trail, slow turnaround
3. **Desired State:** Push notifications, one-click approval, clear history
4. **MVP Features:** Approval workflow, notifications, audit trail, batch approve
