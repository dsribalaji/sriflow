## Engagement Strategy Summary

### Keep Satisfied & Engaged (High Power, High Interest)
*Who:* [Names]
*Approach:* These stakeholders have both the authority to stop the project and the motivation to care about the outcome. Engage early and often. Involve them in decisions that affect their domain. Their sign-off is required before key phase gates.

### Manage Closely (High Power, Low Interest)
*Who:* [Names]
*Approach:* These stakeholders can veto the project but don't follow it closely. Don't overwhelm with detail. Provide clean, brief executive summaries. Flag decisions that require their authority. Surprise is the enemy here — they need to feel informed without being involved daily.

### Keep Informed (Low Power, High Interest)
*Who:* [Names]
*Approach:* These stakeholders are invested in the outcome and often the most useful source of ground-truth feedback. They can't block the project but their adoption determines success. Regular updates, early access to prototypes, and their feedback on usability matters.

### Monitor (Low Power, Low Interest)
*Who:* [Names]
*Approach:* Minimal engagement needed now. Watch for changes — a stakeholder in this quadrant can shift to High Power if their domain becomes relevant mid-project.
